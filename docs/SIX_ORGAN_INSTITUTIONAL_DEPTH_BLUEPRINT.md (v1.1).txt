# Six-Organ Institutional Depth Blueprint

Version: v1.1
Date context: 2026-04-30
Supersedes: v1.0 (2026-04-30 morning)

This file defines the implementation standard for all six opening organs at institutional depth.

Status: accepted ecosystem upgrade track. The current `organ-runtime` surfaces are analysis-mode implementations, and final live authority remains gated by runtime execution truth, funded `Cardia` activation, telemetry calibration, and governance controls.

## 0) Doctrinal Anchor: Biological Fidelity = Operational Standard

The six opening organs are named after biological organs because the bar is the actual function those organs perform in the body, not the metaphor.

1. **Hepar** is named after the liver. The bar is continuous, multi-modal toxin filtration with measurable physiological consequence — translated into protocol-forensic terms as continuous, multi-stage adversarial analysis with measurable precision/recall and bounded false-positive rate.
2. **Cortex** is named after the cerebral cortex. The bar is causal explanation, scenario simulation, and decision-support reasoning — not summary generation.
3. **Synapse** is named after the neural synapse. The bar is selective signal routing with conflict arbitration and capital-sensitive escalation — not message-bus pass-through.
4. **Cardia** is named after the heart. The bar is risk-adjusted allocation with reserve discipline and stress-regime response — not a portfolio rebalancer.
5. **Pneuma** is named after the lungs / breath. The bar is venue-selective execution with cost optimization and feedback to upstream organs — not order routing.
6. **Vox** is named after the voice. The bar is audience-specific narrative packaging with proof-reference linkage and explicit truth-status tagging — not content generation.

The architecture-layer counterpart of MOF §17.8 (Hollow Convergence) applies here: **an organ that fails to perform its biological function at the standard implied by its name is not a depth implementation, it is a name claim**. Name-claims fail Pass/Fail (§4) regardless of how many surfaces are wired up.

This anchor is binding for every organ. Tier-discipline (§3.5) is the operational expression of it.

## 1) Objective

Move each organ from simple role logic to:

1. explicit phase-based processing
2. quantitative scoring and confidence
3. conflict-aware coordination
4. auditable outputs for Data Rail and governance
5. evidence sufficient to clear the §0 biological-fidelity bar at the relevant tier (§3.5)

## 2) Organ Standards

The "Must produce" lines are minimum surfaces. Biological-fidelity language under each organ defines the standard those surfaces must meet to qualify as depth implementations rather than name claims.

### Hepar

Must produce:

1. deterministic forensic risk vectors
2. multi-agent consensus fusion
3. symbolic status integration (`proved_safe` / `counterexample` / `unknown`)
4. action banding and attestation metadata

Biological-fidelity bar: continuous filtration of inbound protocol exposure with measurable precision/recall against an independent labelled incident set, false-positive rate bounded by severity tier, and per-finding evidence linkage. Single-modality analysis (fuzzing-only, static-only, symbolic-only) does not clear the bar. Canonical specification: `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md`. Architecture: `HEPAR_MONTE_CARLO_CONSENSUS_BLUEPRINT.md`.

### Cortex

Must produce:

1. causal drivers (not summary-only)
2. scenario matrix with probabilities and stress posture
3. decision-support recommendations with confidence
4. model-update hooks for recurring failure modes

Biological-fidelity bar: causal explanation that survives adversarial probing — outputs must distinguish correlation from cause, must show scenario probabilities with bounded uncertainty, and must hand off decision-support recommendations with explicit confidence and reasoning trace. Pure summarization does not clear the bar.

### Synapse

Must produce:

1. source trust weighting
2. conflict detection and arbitration routing
3. capital-sensitive escalation behavior
4. route auditability (why signal routed where)

Biological-fidelity bar: selective routing under conflict — when two upstream sources disagree, Synapse must choose, justify the choice in an auditable trace, and escalate capital-sensitive disagreements to governance rather than silently averaging. Pass-through routing does not clear the bar.

### Cardia

Must produce:

1. risk-adjusted allocation scoring
2. reserve-aware deployable budget logic
3. stress-regime actions (crash, liquidity stress, reserve breach)
4. measurable net allocation and blocked-exposure tracking

Biological-fidelity bar: rhythm under stress — Cardia must hold reserve discipline through liquidity stress events, must reduce or block exposure on `crashStressPenalty` regimes (see `ORGAN_INTELLIGENCE_CALIBRATION_REPORT_2026-04-30.md`), and must maintain measurable net allocation tracking that survives audit. A passive rebalancer does not clear the bar. Funded operation remains gated by runtime execution-truth closure.

### Pneuma

Must produce:

1. venue/counterparty filtered execution choices
2. cost-optimized route selection
3. fill ratio + cost metrics
4. feedback signals back into Synapse/Cortex/Cardia

Biological-fidelity bar: selective intake — Pneuma must reject venues failing `minSettlementReliability`, must report fill ratio and cost per execution, and must feed execution outcomes back into upstream organ calibration. Order routing without feedback does not clear the bar.

### Vox

Must produce:

1. audience-specific packages from a common truth set
2. proof-reference linkage
3. narrative truth status (`verified` / `incomplete` / `conflicted`)
4. coherence warnings for operator follow-up

Biological-fidelity bar: speech under truth constraint — every Vox output must carry an explicit truth-status tag, must link to its proof references, and must escalate `incomplete` and `conflicted` states to operator review rather than smoothing them away. Content generation without proof-linkage does not clear the bar.

## 3) Tier Discipline (binding for all six organs)

Every organ output sits at one of three tiers (the architectural counterpart to `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §16):

### 3.1 Advisory tier

1. fixture-verified or staged-data-verified
2. operator-supervised consumption only
3. no external pricing
4. no automated capital action without human-in-the-loop
5. all six organs are at Advisory tier today

### 3.2 Decision-Support tier

1. live-telemetry-verified against a measured horizon
2. precision/recall, false-positive rate, coverage, and time-to-verdict reported per `HEPAR_MONTE_CARLO_CONSENSUS_BLUEPRINT.md` §9 (or organ-equivalent metrics)
3. dispute-and-correction protocol live
4. external pricing allowed only after independent reviewer sign-off
5. automated action allowed within bounded budgets

### 3.3 Authoritative tier

1. multi-cycle live performance evidence
2. independently audited precision/recall and reliability metrics
3. legal carve-outs and insurance in force (Hepar-specific: `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §15)
4. licensing-quality output (Tier 4 revenue surfaces require this)
5. governance override policy in force for conflict escalation

### 3.4 Demotion conditions

An organ demotes one tier on any of:

1. live-telemetry deviation beyond calibrated bounds
2. correction protocol invocation in a measurable window
3. independent reviewer flag
4. governance override of an organ decision

### 3.5 Tier discipline as operational expression of biological fidelity

The §0 anchor and the §3 ladder are the same rule from two angles. **A claim that an organ has cleared the biological-fidelity bar is a tier claim.** Advisory tier means "the organ can plausibly perform its biological function in fixtures." Decision-Support tier means "the organ has demonstrated it on live telemetry." Authoritative tier means "the organ has demonstrated it across cycles with independent verification." No organ is biological-fidelity-cleared in the abstract; it is cleared at a tier, with evidence at that tier, and the §16-equivalent gate must be passed before that tier is claimed externally.

## 4) Pass/Fail Definition (Per Organ)

An organ is **Institutional-Depth PASS** when:

1. it emits structured outputs with confidence/decision state
2. test coverage validates key edge cases
3. outputs can be tied to evidence records
4. orchestration captures interactions with at least two other organs
5. outputs meet the §0 biological-fidelity bar at a declared tier (§3)

An organ is **FAIL** when:

1. it is only static role logic without adaptive scoring, auditable reasoning, or cross-organ integration; **or**
2. it claims a tier (§3) without the evidence required at that tier; **or**
3. its surfaces wire up but do not perform the biological function the organ name claims (a name-claim under §0).
