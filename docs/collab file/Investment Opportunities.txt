For each opportunity: pros, cons, and potential ROI/return drivers
1.	Immediate commercialization of Tier 3 forensic reports (NDA advisory forensic engagements)
•	Pros
•	Fast time to revenue: Tier 3 is explicitly called out as an acceptable NDA advisory product and a pragmatic speed‑to‑revenue path.
•	Low barrier to sell compared with waiting for full organ‑set maturity; fits existing market demand (protocols urgently need audits/forensics).
•	Differentiation: HEPAR’s continuous, multimodal, wallet‑graph‑aware analysis can be pitched as superior to static audits.
•	Captures market of protocols who prioritize rapid containment of exploit risk and are willing to pay for deep forensic expertise under NDA.
•	Cons / Risks
•	Messaging currently too jargon heavy; sales friction unless materials are translated to plain B2B pain‑point language.
•	Reliance on NDA work limits public marketing and social proof; slow to build brand trust if most engagements are private.
•	Operational capacity risk: delivery depends on complex internal tooling and skilled analysts—scaling could be hard and costly.
•	Legal/liability exposure unless attestation and contract terms are tightly defined.
•	Potential ROI / Return drivers
•	Near‑term revenue per engagement could be significant (premium forensic pricing vs standard audits) because clients pay for emergency/high‑value remediation and forensic clarity.
•	Revenue converts quickly to cash; customer relationships can lead to repeat business and upsell to longer engagements.
•	ROI improves if sales materials are repackaged to highlight direct cost savings (developer hours saved, reduced false positives) and liability reduction.
•	Conservative estimate: if priced at typical premium forensic rates and closing a handful of NDA engagements per quarter, early revenue could cover runway and fund organ‑set R&D; exact ROI depends on pricing, close rates, and fulfillment cost.

1.	Selling on‑chain attestation and multi‑stage analytical stack as a liability‑reduction product (value to protocols, insurers, and underwriters)
•	Pros
•	Strong differentiated moat: on‑chain cryptographic proof of simulation/execution (Stage D) and multi‑stage evidence trail is a persuasive commercial asset against legacy auditors’ static PDFs.

•	Requires legal & compliance work to translate technical attestations into recognized contractual protections (insurer acceptance, recognized standards).
•	Some clients or insurers may be slow to adopt new attestation models; time to market for insurance integrations can be long.
•	Technical maturity and independent reviewer acceptance are necessary; without recognized third‑party validators the attestation may carry less weight.
•	Potential regulatory or evidentiary questions about on‑chain proofs in legal disputes.
•	Potential ROI / Return drivers
•	Large upside: if insurers accept on‑chain attestations as risk mitigation, HEPAR can command premium recurring fees, integrations with insurance products, and enterprise SLAs.
•	Revenue is scalable via SaaS/monitoring subscriptions plus per‑engagement attestation fees.
•	ROI is highest if HEPAR secures pilot programs with insurers or large protocols (proof points accelerate market adoption and justify premium pricing).
