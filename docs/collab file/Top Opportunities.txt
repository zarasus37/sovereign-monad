1.	Map four-stage analytical stack to commercial assurance and liability reduction
•	Scores: Impact 8, Feasibility 7, Strategic alignment 9 → Total 24/30
•	Rationale: Converts technical moat into concrete commercial benefits (reduced false positives, evidence for insurers). High strategic value but requires careful legal framing and marketing.
•	Roadmap (10–14 weeks, parallelizable with #1 & #2)
•	Week 0–2: Workshop with engineering, legal, and sales to align what each stage (A–D) demonstrably achieves for a client.
•	Week 2–6: Produce “Assurance Map” documents that link:
•	Stage A: static forensics → immediate vulnerability identification and patch prioritization (time-to-fix metrics).
•	Stage B: bounded symbolic proving → formal guarantees about specific classes of vulnerabilities (reduction in exploit likelihood).
•	Stage C: multi-agent execution → lower false positives and simulated exploit scenarios (developer hours saved).
•	Stage D: consensus fusion & on-chain attestation → verifiable proof-of-simulation for insurers and auditors (liability reduction).
•	Week 6–10: Integrate these claims into sales collateral, RFP responses, and insurance/partnership conversations; produce data-backed examples or synthetic benchmarks.
•	Week 10–14: Engage 1–2 insurer or institutional partners for pilot underwriting conversations using the on-chain attestation as evidence.
