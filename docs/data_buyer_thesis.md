## Product 1 — Quant Behavior Factor Feed

**What it is:**
Per-period behavioral factors extracted from agent populations:
- Overconfidence Index (position size vs. signal strength)
- Discipline Score (deviation from stated strategy under stress)
- Panic Threshold (drawdown % that triggers abort)
- Contrarian Tendency (correlated vs. anti-correlated action to regime)
- Recovery Aggressiveness (sizing behavior after drawdown)

**Buyer:** Quantitative hedge funds, systematic trading desks

**Why they buy:**
Behavioral alpha factors are an active research area. AQR, Man Group, and Two Sigma all publish on sentiment and behavioral anomalies. The problem: existing behavioral datasets are self-reported surveys, lab experiments, or inferred from aggregated retail order flow — all noisy, indirect proxies.

This dataset is **direct behavioral observation under real economic stakes**, segmented by validated personality profile. That's a materially different signal class.

**Price signal:** Alternative data subscriptions at quant funds range from $50K–$500K/year depending on exclusivity and update frequency. Factor feeds without exclusivity sit in the $20K–$100K range.

**First buyer path:** Academic quant finance research lab (lower price, high credibility signal → easier institutional intro path)

## Product 2 — Behavioral Finance Research Feed

**What it is:**
Anonymized aggregate reports published on a cadence:
- Personality cluster performance by regime type
- Trait-performance correlations across market cycles
- Behavioral biases expressed by specific trait combinations
- Cross-personality coordination patterns in shared market environments

**Buyer:** Academic behavioral finance researchers, institutional asset managers with research arms, fintech companies building personality-aligned products

**Why they buy:**
The foundational behavioral finance datasets — Odean (trading records), Barber (gender studies), Kahneman lab experiments — are decades old, limited in scope, and not connected to psychometric profiles.

A live, growing, longitudinally tracked dataset connecting Big Five + HEXACO to real trading decisions is **publishable, citable, and licensable**. Research institutions will pay for access and co-publication rights.

**Price signal:** Dataset access licenses to research institutions: $5K–$50K/year. Co-publication arrangements can involve upfront fees or revenue share from resulting IP.

**First buyer path:** Cold outreach to behavioral finance faculty at top-10 finance programs (Wharton, Chicago Booth, Columbia). Offer a pilot dataset for a named research collaboration. This generates academic credibility that pulls institutional buyers inward.

## Product 3 — Regime Response Reports

**What it is:**
Event-triggered reports published after significant market regimes:
- How each personality cluster responded to a specific shock (crash, rally, volatility spike, liquidity crisis)
- Aggregate decision timing, sizing, and abort behavior by trait profile
- Predictive utility: which profiles led vs. lagged in recognizing regime shifts

**Buyer:** Macro hedge funds, risk management consultancies, financial advisor platforms

**Why they buy:**
Regime response data is valuable for stress-testing portfolio construction assumptions. Knowing that High Neuroticism + Low Conscientiousness agents panic-sold at 15% drawdown in 2026's Q2 volatility spike — with that tied to a specific trait cluster — is actionable for any firm trying to model client behavior or construct tail-risk hedges.

**Price signal:** Event-triggered reports sold as one-time purchases ($500–$5,000 per report) or bundled into subscription ($15K–$80K/year for ongoing access). Risk management consulting firms often resell into client engagements at 3–5x.

**First buyer path:** Risk analytics platforms (Bloomberg's risk tools, Axioma, MSCI) as data enrichment partners. They already have institutional distribution — adding behavioral layer to their existing risk data is a natural upsell.

## Product 4 — Oracle API (Behavioral Intelligence Tier)

**What it is:**
B2B SaaS API with two tiers:
- Base: Market risk evaluation, position sizing recommendations
- Premium (personality-informed): Input a trait vector + market state, output return distribution + behavioral risk flags specific to that personality profile

**Buyer:** DeFi protocols building automated risk management, MEV desks, robo-advisory platforms, gaming operators managing player risk

**Why they buy:**
A risk API that accounts for *who* is making the decision — not just what the market is doing — is a genuinely novel product. Robo-advisors currently use risk questionnaires that are self-reported and static. An API that adjusts risk recommendations based on a validated psychometric profile expressed through actual behavior is a defensible upgrade.

**Price signal:** Per-call API pricing ($0.001–$0.01/call), subscription tiers ($500–$5,000/month), enterprise rev-share for high-volume clients. Standard B2B SaaS multiples apply.

**First buyer path:** DeFi protocols already managing protocol-owned liquidity or user vault strategies. They need position sizing and risk models and don't have research teams to build them. Start with one reference client at discounted rate in exchange for a public case study.

## Product 5 — External Agent Framework Licensing

**What it is:**
White-label licensing of the personality-agent runtime stack:
- Psychometric onboarding pipeline
- Trait vector → behavioral parameter translation layer
- Agent behavioral engine with configurable guardrails
- DCS scoring engine for internal data reward systems

**Buyer:** Other DeFi protocols wanting to implement personality-driven agents, fintech platforms building personalized investment tools, enterprise HR/organizational behavior platforms

**Why they buy:**
Building a psychometrically validated agent system from scratch requires significant behavioral science expertise that most engineering teams don't have. Licensing a runtime and research stack that already has meaningful repo implementation behind it is faster and cheaper than internal development, but canonical live-status claims should come from the MOF rather than this thesis document.

**Price signal:** Framework licensing in B2B software: $50K–$500K upfront + rev-share or annual maintenance. Enterprise SaaS with customization can command higher.

**First buyer path:** DeFi protocols in adjacent spaces (prediction markets, automated portfolio management) that want agent differentiation without building the behavioral science stack.

## Product 6 — Trait-Gated Protocol Fees

**What it is:**
Access to empirically discovered strategy classes gated behind personality trait thresholds — users pay a fee to access protocols that their trait profile qualifies them for.

Example: "Storm Detector Protocol" — only accessible to agents with verified High Conscientiousness + High Neuroticism profile, because only that trait combination can execute its zero-deviation requirements safely.

**Why this is novel:**
This is the first financial protocol where access is governed by *who you are psychologically*, not just capital or token holdings. The data rail proves the trait requirement is empirically justified — it's not gatekeeping, it's safety calibration backed by behavioral evidence.

**Price signal:** Protocol access fees can be structured as one-time unlock, per-use fees, or performance rev-share. Trait-gated exclusivity creates real pricing power.
