This document inherits two doctrinal anchors from the v2.0 specification and the supporting blueprints:

1. **Biological Fidelity = Operational Standard** (`HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §0, `SIX_ORGAN_INSTITUTIONAL_DEPTH_BLUEPRINT.md` §0). Hepar is named after the liver. The product bar is the function the liver actually performs in the body — continuous, multi-modal toxin filtration with measurable physiological consequences — translated into protocol-forensic terms. "Liver-grade" is a literal performance and evidence claim, not a marketing flourish.
2. **Tier Discipline** (`HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §16). Every commercial output sits at one of three tiers: **Advisory**, **Decision-Support**, or **Authoritative**. The tier governs what the customer is allowed to rely on, what KPI gates must be cleared before externalization, and what legal and disclosure posture applies. Pricing without tier discipline is overclaim by default.

## 1) Hepar Forensic Stack (v2)

The packaging in this section is a summary view. The full architecture — Stage A static forensics, Stage B bounded symbolic proving, Stage C multi-agent Monte Carlo, Stage D consensus fusion — and the full per-vector scoring formula `risk_v = severity_v × (0.55·consensus_v + 0.25·repro_v + 0.20·proof_term_v)` are specified in `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §4 and `HEPAR_MONTE_CARLO_CONSENSUS_BLUEPRINT.md`.

Hepar evaluates opportunities through five explicit forensic surfaces (the "what is examined") backed by the four-stage analytical stack (the "how it is examined"):

1. Bytecode Privilege Checks
2. Proxy-Admin Control Analysis
3. LP Unlock Concentration and Timeline
4. Wallet Graph Taint and Behavioral Patterning
5. Adversarial Execution Simulation

A sixth **Economic Viability** surface and a seventh **Composite** surface are defined in `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §4.2 (7-Dimensional Risk Framework). The seventh dimension is required to be evaluated as a **score band + top-vector check**, never a single scalar. This rule is binding.

### Layer outputs

Hepar returns:

1. hard-block reasons
2. weighted risk vector (7 dimensions)
3. aggregate risk score with explicit decision band (`ALLOW`, `GUARDED ALLOW`, `RESTRICTED`, `DENY`, `HARD_BLOCK`)
4. confidence level (`low`, `medium`, `high`) derived from consensus rate, reproducibility, and proof-term coverage
5. detailed reasons for operator review and downstream narrative packaging
6. on-chain attestation metadata (`heparRunId`, `evidenceRoot`, `postedOnChain`)

### Hard-block examples

1. unchecked owner-drain functions
2. selfdestruct reachable
3. uninitialized proxy implementation
4. single-actor upgrade path with timelock bypass
5. concentrated near-term LP unlock cliff
6. creator wallet taint + repeated rug pattern
7. adversarial invariant break with material exploit estimate

## 2) Six-Organ Productization Logic

Commercial value is treated as packaging of existing organ outputs:

1. `Synapse` routes signal and urgency.
2. `Hepar` produces forensic risk truth.
3. `Cortex` synthesizes explainable institutional intelligence.
4. `Vox` packages into report/feed/public forms.
5. `Pneuma` converts outbound distribution and lead flow.
6. `Cardia` preserves treasury rhythm and reinvestment discipline.

No additional doctrine claim is needed. The revenue surface is already implicit in current runtime behavior. Packaging never invents new authority — every commercial output inherits the tier of the underlying organ output.

## 3) Revenue Tiers (Optimized Packaging)

> All numbers in this section are **planning bands**, not forecasts and not guarantees. They are sized to the addressable institutional risk-data market and assume: clean tier-discipline progression to Decision-Support or Authoritative, paid pilots that convert, and successful disclosure protocol execution. None of these prices may be quoted externally until the activation gates in `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §16 for the relevant tier are passed.

### Tier 1: Hepar Risk API

- product: continuous protocol surveillance
- pricing shape (planning band): `$0.01/check` usage lane or `$50k/mo` unlimited commit lane for institutional integrators
- positioning: an exploration-tier retail/SMB lane (`$99-$999/mo` self-serve) is intentionally **not part of v2.1**. A retail surface multiplies legal exposure (BLACK-tier protocols, defamation, CCPA/GDPR data-subject rights) before independent reviewer sign-off and insurance are in place. Retail surface is gated behind `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §15 (Legal Posture) reaching its full minimum and Decision-Support tier metrics.
- primary organ producers: `Hepar`, `Synapse`, `Vox`, `Pneuma`
- KPI gates required before external pricing (Decision-Support tier — see §16):
  1. ≥30 days continuous fixture-based regression with no false-positive HARD_BLOCK
  2. detection precision and recall vs an independent labelled incident set, reported to §9 of `HEPAR_MONTE_CARLO_CONSENSUS_BLUEPRINT.md`
  3. dispute-and-correction protocol live (see §15 of v2.0 spec)
  4. independent reviewer sign-off for at least one BLACK-tier publication

### Tier 2: Institutional Risk Feed

- product: live risk intelligence feed for funds and market makers
- pricing shape (planning band): `$50k-$500k/mo` based on latency, coverage, and support tier
- primary organ producers: `Hepar`, `Cortex`, `Vox`, `Pneuma`
- KPI gates required before external pricing (Decision-Support tier minimum, Authoritative tier preferred — see §16):
  1. institutional pilot conversion ≥1 paid pilot with documented hit-rate vs realized incidents
  2. retention/churn evidence over a measured horizon
  3. signal hit-rate vs realized incidents reported in the customer's own portfolio terms
  4. on-chain attestation pattern live (`heparRunId`, `evidenceRoot`)
  5. SLA documentation including correction-window commitments

### Tier 3: Protocol Forensic Report

- product: deep pre-mainnet forensic workup
- pricing shape (planning band): `$5k` basic report / `$25k` institutional deep-dive + remediation retest upsell
- primary organ producers: `Hepar`, `Cortex`, `Vox`
- KPI gates required before external pricing (Advisory tier acceptable for pre-mainnet engagements; finding severity gated by §15):
  1. turnaround time, close rate per outbound lead, retest upsell conversion
  2. report template signed off by independent reviewer (legal + technical)
  3. customer NDAs covering pre-disclosure findings
  4. coordinated-disclosure protocol applied per `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §10

Tier 3 fast-lane operational polish (target `95+` readiness):

1. automated report templating
2. quality checklist + sign-off workflow
3. automated evidence packaging with proof-link export
4. customer portal intake with 24-hour target turnaround
5. explicit SLA guarantees for response-time and quality commitment

### Tier 4: Insurance Underwriting Dataset

- product: licensing-quality protocol risk dataset
- pricing shape (planning band): annual licensing first (`$10M-$50M`) with optional premium-share lane (`2-5%`) after actuarial trust is proven
- primary organ producers: `Hepar`, `Cortex`, `Synapse`, `Cardia`, `Pneuma`
- KPI gates required before external pricing (Authoritative tier required — see §16):
  1. partner underwriter count ≥1 with signed data-license terms
  2. refresh SLA met across at least one full annual cycle of fixture-and-live data
  3. insured loss-ratio improvement vs baseline reported and independently reviewed
  4. actuarial-grade reproducibility (`repro_v`) and proof-term coverage (`proof_term_v`) per `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §4
  5. legal carve-outs for warranty-of-fitness and limitations-of-liability

## 4) Recommended Launch Order

Execution order optimized for speed-to-revenue and de-risking the enterprise sales cycle:

1. Tier 3 (Forensic Reports) — lowest legal and operational surface; pre-mainnet engagements; Advisory tier acceptable
2. Tier 1 (Hepar API, institutional commit lane only) — recurring revenue; requires Decision-Support tier
3. Tier 2 (Institutional Feed) — higher revenue, requires retention and pilot evidence
4. Tier 4 (Insurance Dataset) — Authoritative tier required, longest cycle, highest ceiling

Note: this order revises the prior v2.0 launch order (Tier 1 first). Tier 3 should lead because the Advisory-tier surface is acceptable and the legal exposure is contained by NDA and pre-disclosure protocol.

## 5) Implementation Boundaries

1. Output remains bounded by governance, rights review, and activation scope.

6. No BLACK-tier publication may occur before `HEPAR_v2.0_SPECIFICATION.md` §15 (Legal Posture) is fully in force, including independent reviewer sign-off, insurance, dispute protocol, and correction protocol.

## 7) Consensus Extension (Current Build Surface)

Hepar has a multi-agent consensus scaffold in `organ-runtime`:

1. ingest parallel agent findings per protocol campaign
2. fuse consensus rate, reproducibility, and symbolic status into a bounded risk score
3. emit action bands (`allow`, `guarded_allow`, `restricted`, `deny`, `hard_block`)
4. produce compact attestation metadata (`heparRunId`, `evidenceRoot`, `postedOnChain`)

This remains an analysis-mode evidence surface and does not by itself grant live operating authority. The consensus extension is the runtime expression of `HEPAR_MONTE_CARLO_CONSENSUS_BLUEPRINT.md` Stages C and D.
