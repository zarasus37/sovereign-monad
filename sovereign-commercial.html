The Problem
Most cross-chain bots fail
in the same three places
  • LAT
    Bridge delay treated as a fee problem
    Bridge windows create price-path risk. Treating bridge delay as a fixed fee misses the core exposure: spread can decay to zero or invert during transit.
  • SIZE
    Fixed-percentage position sizing
    Sizing without reference to edge-versus-variance systematically either under- or over-deploys capital, leaving performance on the table or blowing risk limits.
  • TAIL
    Raw spread thresholds without tail modeling
    A 20 bps spread with high correlation and a 90-second bridge window behaves very differently from the same spread with low correlation and a 6-second bridge. Raw thresholds cannot tell them apart.
  • EV
    Effective spread with volatility decay
    The RiskGnosisEngine discounts raw spread by volatility decay over the bridge window, compounds that penalty, and subtracts fixed execution costs in basis points.
  • KEL
    Fractional Kelly position sizing
    Size is derived from edge relative to variance, then capped at 10% of the portfolio. You deploy exactly what the opportunity justifies, no more and no less.
  • MC
    Monte Carlo tail-risk simulation
    Correlated GBM across both chains, log-normal bridge latency, explicit bridge-failure probability, and gas fees produce EV, variance, Sharpe score, and tail-loss estimates per opportunity.
How It Works
From raw spread to evaluated decision
in a single API call
Send a spread signal. Get back an approve-or-reject decision with explicit sizing, EV, Sharpe, and tail loss, all derived from the full Monte Carlo engine.
POST https://sovereign-rge-api.sovereign-mev.workers.dev/evaluate
STEP 01
Spread Input
Submit raw spread in bps, liquidity depth, bridge window, chain pair, and asset volatility. The engine reads your parameters against its threshold matrix.
STEP 02
Risk Evaluation
Effective spread is computed. Then 1,000 correlated Monte Carlo paths are simulated across both chains with real bridge-failure and latency modeling.
STEP 03
Decision Output
You receive approve/reject, fractional Kelly size, expected value, Sharpe score, tail-loss estimate, and the threshold distances that drove the decision.
