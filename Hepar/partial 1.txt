partial 2

Hepar is the Forensic Intelligence Organ of the Sovereign Monad ecosystem. Named after the biological liver, its primary role is to act as a continuous, multi-modal risk filtration system for DeFi protocols and centralized exchanges.

While traditional security companies provide a single, static "snapshot" audit that quickly becomes outdated, Hepar is designed to provide continuous, dynamic defense. It constantly ingests real-time data from platforms like DefiLlama, Snapshot, Tally, and direct on-chain telemetry to identify threats, validate opportunities, and deliver actionable intelligence before a protocol's Total Value Locked (TVL) can be drained.

Here is a detailed breakdown of exactly what Hepar does during its auditing and risk-filtration process.

The Four-Stage Forensic Analytical Stack
Hepar processes inbound smart contracts and protocol data through a rigorous, automated pipeline designed to reduce false positives, save developer remediation time, and generate verifiable cryptographic proofs.

Stage A — Static Forensics (Deterministic Prefilter)
This is the initial filtration layer. It rapidly scans the protocol for known, deterministic risk vectors.

Bytecode privilege analysis: Checking for dangerous administrative powers.

Proxy and Upgrade mapping: Identifying who holds the keys to upgrade the contract and how those upgrades are authorized.

Economic/Liquidity analysis: Tracking LP (Liquidity Provider) unlock concentration and timelines.

Wallet Graph Taint: Analyzing associated wallets for behavioral anomalies or ties to known malicious actors.

Stage B — Bounded Symbolic Proving
Moving beyond simple pattern matching, this stage uses mathematical proofs to verify the protocol's safety.

It formulates targeted symbolic constraints around critical smart contract invariants (e.g., authorization, upgradeability, accounting math, and reentrancy states).

The engine attempts to mathematically prove that the contract is safe, or it generates a specific "counterexample" (a reproducible path showing exactly how an invariant can be broken).

Stage C — Multi-Agent Monte Carlo Adversarial Execution
If a protocol survives the math, it is subjected to simulated attacks. Hepar deploys five specialized AI agents that run parallel stochastic (randomized but guided) campaigns against the contract:

Hepar-Privilege Agent: Attempts to escalate privileges and hijack admin roles.

Hepar-Arithmetic Agent: Probes for integer overflows, underflows, and precision loss.

Hepar-Reentrancy Agent: Attempts callback vulnerabilities to drain funds.

Hepar-Economic Agent: Looks for MEV (Maximal Extractable Value) vulnerabilities, frontrunning, and flash-loan exploits.

Hepar-State Agent: Tries to force the contract into inconsistent internal states.

Stage D — Consensus Fusion & Attestation
The final stage gathers all the intelligence from the previous three stages.

Consensus Fusion: It aggregates the findings, normalizes the risk vectors, and assigns a final Risk Band/Decision (e.g., ALLOW, RESTRICTED, HARDBLOCK).

On-Chain Attestation: This is Hepar's unique competitive moat. It generates an immutable cryptographic proof (an attestation payload) of the entire simulation and forensic run. This provides insurers, legal teams, and investors with verifiable, on-chain evidence that the protocol was rigorously tested at a specific point in time.

Hepar's Role in the Broader Ecosystem
Once Hepar completes its 4-stage filtration, it passes the "purified" intelligence to the rest of the Sovereign Monad ecosystem. For example, it tells Cardia (the capital allocation organ) exactly what the risk-adjusted limits should be, and it alerts Synapse (the routing organ) if an emergency hard-block needs to be instituted.
